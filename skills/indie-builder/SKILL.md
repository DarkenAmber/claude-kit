# Indie Builder - Claude Skill

> One person. One product. Real revenue.
> Build something people want, charge for it, and grow it without burning out.

---

## When to Use This Skill

Use when:
- Building a micro-SaaS or indie product from scratch
- Deciding what to build, how to price it, how to distribute it
- Validating an idea before investing weeks of development
- Figuring out why your product is not growing
- Making product decisions as a solo developer

Do NOT use when:
- Building enterprise software with a team
- Seeking VC funding or building for acquisition
- The goal is a hobby project with no revenue intent
- You need technical architecture advice (use other skills for that)

---

## Core Philosophy

A micro-SaaS is a small software business that one person can build, run, and grow.

The goal is not to build the best product. The goal is to build the right product for the right people and charge them fairly for the value it creates.

Revenue is not the enemy of creativity. It is what lets you keep building.

---

## The Indie Builder Stack

```
Idea → Validation → MVP → First Revenue → Growth → Sustainability
```

Never skip validation. Never optimize growth before first revenue.

---

## Rules

### 1. Niche down until it hurts
- "Project management tool" is not a niche
- "Project management for freelance electricians in CIS" is a niche
- The narrower the niche, the easier to find users and the less competition
- You can always expand later - you cannot easily narrow later

### 2. Charge from day one
- Free plans attract users who never pay
- A product people pay for is a product people use
- Start with a simple one-time payment before building subscription logic
- $9/month is easier to justify than $99/year even if they are the same

### 3. Distribution before features
- Most indie products die from lack of distribution, not lack of features
- Before building feature #2, ask: how will people find feature #1?
- Build in public - share progress, failures, numbers
- One distribution channel that works beats ten that might

### 4. Talk to users before building
- 5 conversations with real users are worth more than 50 hours of building
- Ask what they do today, not what they want
- Watch them use your product - do not explain it
- If nobody will talk to you about the problem, nobody will pay to solve it

### 5. One metric that matters
- Pick one number that defines success right now
- Do not optimize everything at once

**Subscription model:**
- Early stage: number of people who paid at least once
- Growth stage: monthly recurring revenue (MRR)
- Maturity: churn rate

**One-time / license model:**
- Early stage: conversion rate (free to paid)
- Growth stage: units sold per month
- Maturity: LTV and repeat purchases / upsells

## Validation Before Building

Before writing code, answer these:

| Question | If you cannot answer - stop |
|----------|-----------------------------|
| Who exactly has this problem? | You do not have a target user |
| How do they solve it today? | You do not know your competition |
| Why would they pay for your solution? | You do not have a value proposition |
| How will you reach them? | You do not have a distribution plan |
| What does success look like in 90 days? | You do not have a goal |

---

## Pricing Framework

### Start simple
- One product, one price
- No free SaaS tier until you have 100+ paying customers
- Exception: hardware-gated or feature-limited demo is fine as a funnel
  (free tier that is useless without your device/license = conversion tool, not a leak)
- No annual plans until monthly is working

### Price on value, not cost
- What is it worth to the user? Not: how long did it take to build?
- If it saves 2 hours/week at $50/hour - it is worth $400/month
- Charge 10-20% of the value you create
- These numbers are illustrative for high-income markets.
  Adjust for your market and local purchasing power.

### One-time / license pricing
```
Free demo    limited features or hardware-gated
Pro unlock   one-time payment, full features
```
Works well for: mobile apps, desktop tools, hardware companions, utilities.

### Subscription pricing tiers (when ready)
```
Solo      $X/month  - 1 user, core features
Team      $3X/month - 5 users, collaboration
Business  $8X/month - unlimited users, priority support
```

Start with Solo only. Add tiers when users ask for them.

## Distribution Channels

Pick ONE and go deep before adding another:

| Channel | Best for | Time to first result |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| Build in public (Twitter/X) | Developer tools, indie products | 3-6 months |
| Dev.to / Hashnode articles | Technical tools | 1-3 months |
| Reddit (niche subreddits) | Niche B2C tools | Days to weeks |
| Telegram channels / groups | Mobile apps, B2C tools, niche communities | Days to weeks |
| Product Hunt launch | New product launch | 1 day spike |
| Play Store ASO | Mobile apps - organic search | 1-3 months |
| SEO / content | Long-term sustainable | 6-12 months |
| Cold outreach | B2B, specific niche | Weeks |
| YouTube / TikTok | Consumer products | 3-6 months |

### Build in public formula
```
Progress update + honest number + lesson learned = engaging post
```
Example: "Week 3: 0 → 3 paying users. Lesson: free users never convert. Removed free tier."

## Launch Checklist

Before announcing to the world:

- [ ] Landing page with clear value proposition (one sentence)
- [ ] Pricing visible without signing up
- [ ] Payment working end-to-end (test with real card)
- [ ] At least 3 people outside your network have used it
- [ ] You can describe who it is for in one sentence
- [ ] Support email or chat exists
- [ ] Basic analytics in place (know where users come from)

---

## Growth Killers (avoid these)

- Building features instead of talking to users
- Optimizing conversion before having traffic
- Adding a free tier to "grow faster"
- Rewriting the tech stack instead of selling
- Comparing MRR to funded startups
- Waiting until it is "ready" to launch
- Solving your own problem nobody else has

---

## Sustainability Rules

A sustainable indie business:
- Does not require you to work 80 hours/week
- Does not depend on one customer for more than 20% of revenue
- Has enough MRR to cover your costs before you optimize growth
- Can survive you taking 2 weeks off

If any of these are false - fix it before adding features.

## Revenue Milestones

### Subscription model
| Milestone | What it means | What to do next |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| $1 MRR | Someone paid | Find 9 more like them |
| $100 MRR | Signal that value exists | Double down on what got you here |
| $1K MRR | Ramen profitable | Cut churn before growing |
| $5K MRR | Real business | Hire first help or automate |
| $10K MRR | Freedom | Now you can think about scale |

### One-time / license model
| Milestone | What it means | What to do next |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| First sale | Someone paid | Understand exactly who and why |
| 10 sales | Pattern exists | Find more people like your first 10 |
| 100 sales | Product-market fit signal | Optimize conversion funnel |
| 1000 sales | Real business | Add upsell, new tier, or companion product |

---

## What to Build Next

When deciding on the next feature:

| Question | Weight |
|----------|--------|
| Did 3+ paying users ask for it? | High |
| Does it reduce churn? | High |
| Does it help acquisition? | Medium |
| Is it cool and you want to build it? | Low |

If the answer to the first two is no - do not build it yet.

---

## Anti-Patterns

- Building a "platform" before having 10 customers
- Spending more on tools than you earn in revenue
- Adding team features before having solo users
- Raising prices only when desperate
- Ignoring churn and focusing only on new users
- Building a complex app before validating that people will pay for the core value
- Hiring before the business can afford it
